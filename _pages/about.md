Hi!  

I’m **Kacper Kapuśniak**, a 3rd year **PhD student in Machine Learning** at the **University of Oxford**, supervised by [Professor Michael Bronstein](https://www.cs.ox.ac.uk/people/michael.bronstein/). My research focuses on **generative AI methods**, **geometric deep learning**, and their **applications in chemistry and biology**.  


Most recently, I interned with the BioEmu team at Microsoft Research, where I worked on flow-based few-step generative models for sampling all-atom protein equilibrium ensembles. I have also interned at Valence Labs, Recursion, where I worked on *flow matching for molecular dynamics* and improving structural diversity of generated protein ensamble.

Before Oxford, I completed an **MSc in Data Science** at **ETH Zürich**, where my thesis explored self-supervised graph neural network approaches. I also hold a BEng in Electrical Engineering from **UCL**. Along the way, I have interned in **software engineering**, including machine learning engineering at Graphcore.
